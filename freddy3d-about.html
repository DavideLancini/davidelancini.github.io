The Freddy3D Project

My Journey

The first 3D printer I bought was a prusa clone kit by Geeetech. The machined had structural problems given by the wood assembly of the z axis. By studying engineering I learned how to overcame the problems with the frame. I came out with the idea to build a printer that can be deployed in an home environment and build a service that can give tecnical support. I couldn't use the prusa project so I decided to design a printer that can be easily used by normies.

Modularity was a priority. I started to build my first prototype around 6 main components. The first is the structure. I developed a cubic frame made by 2020 standard aluminium extrusion. The joints were made firstly in ABS, designed to accomodate a stepper motor and the mechanism that connect to the other components. The second macro-component is the z-axis. This structrure accomodate the floating heated bed and is connected only to the main structure. This axis is not connected to the others and give the option to expandthe overall lenght of the machine to accomodate bigger prints. The x-axis and the y-axis are connected to the frame and to the extrusion block. This decision was made to reduce the dead width of the machine. This solution also remove the weight dependency on one axis to the other as they are both connected to the frame improving the rigidity. The extrusion block is vertically connected to the x-y joint. This facilitate the nozzle change as the entire block easily came out. The cable nest Is static for all the axis. The only moving cable is the one that need to reach the E-axis. It is parallel to the filament and moves only on 2 axis as the Z is inverted and is controlled from below the bed. The motion controller is positioned under the bed near the PSU.
